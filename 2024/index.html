2024 Republican Primary Drop Out Prediction

- A contest hosted by Oliver Gladfelter -

N Republican candidates have entered the presidential race. N-1 candidates will concede to the party's eventual nominee. Can you correctly predict the order in which these candidates will drop out?

Complete this form by November 15th, 2023 to enter the contest. Any candidates who drop out before then will be removed from the scoring formula, so you won't be penalized if you've already submitted your entry form.

Competition ends no later than June 2024. See scoring details here.


Remember, you're predicting candidate drop out order. Contest scoring will not consider final delegate counts unless multiple candidates have not ended their campaigns going into the 2024 convention. Recall Marco Rubio won more delegates than John Kasich in the 2016 Republican primary, but still ended his campaign earlier - so try to consider how stubborn each candidate might be!

General Rules

Scoring

The player with the lowest Kendall tau distance wins.

Kendall tau distance compares how similar any two ordered lists are: the number of adjacent swaps needed to make a player's prediction match the real order equals their Kendall tau distance. Tie Breaker

Final scoring will be calculated once the final drop out order and 2024 Republican nominee are known. If there is a tie for 1st, 2nd, or 3rd place, ties will be broken by omitting the first candidate who dropped out and rerunning the scoring algorithm. The player with the lower Kendall tau distance from that will be awarded the better final placement. If their Kendall tau distances continue to match, this process will be repeated iteratively until we reach a decisive outcome.

In other words, ties are broken by determining which player more accurately predicted the drop out positions of the final N - X candidates, where N is the number of candidates and X is the number of early-drop-out candidates needed to omit in order to break the tie. So accurate predictions closer to the end of the drop out order are more important than correctly guessing the earlier drop outs for a tie breaker.

Brokered Convention

A brokered convention occurs when no single candidate wins a majority of delegates through the standard primary process, flinging the nomination process into a series of deals and revotes. In this rare scenario, we could have multiple candidates actively campaigning right up to the end.

A candidate may suspend their campaign during a brokered convention, in between convention votes. In this event, their withdraw from the primary is considered a standard drop out for the purposes of this contest and they are simply added to their place in the drop out order.

However, if a candidate may also decide to fight all the way to the bitter end and eventually lose the nomination through a vote. In this case, their campaign is considered over and they are added to the drop out order. If multiple candidates meet this fate, the candidate receiving fewer final delegates is considered the earlier drop out. This rule may apply even in the absence of a brokered convention if there is a protest candidate whom refuses to concede during the primary.
